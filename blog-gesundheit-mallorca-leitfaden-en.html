Health security on Mallorca: the guide for residents and families

Angel GarciaICU nurse · 20+ years8 min read

Moving to Mallorca or keeping a second home here is a gain in quality of life. What many people organise too late is their own medical security — until, in an emergency, language, system and time are all missing at once. This guide brings structure to it, calmly and practically.

1. Public or private: understanding the Spanish system

Spain has a capable public health system (Sistema Nacional de Salud) and a strong private sector. As an EU citizen, the European Health Insurance Card (EHIC) gives you access to medically necessary care in the public system. For planned treatments, doctors of your choice and private clinics — and for short routes without waiting lists — private international or supplementary insurance is usually sensible.

The difference shows less in an emergency (which is covered everywhere) than in everyday care: appointments, specialists, continuity. Those who know both worlds combine them wisely.

2. Finding the right doctors and clinics

The island has excellent private clinics and many multilingual doctors. The challenge is rarely quality — it is orientation: which specialist, which clinic, which route? Three principles help:

  • A fixed, trusted point of contact instead of searching anew every time.
  • Professional rather than merely literal translation — a medical term translated wrongly can have consequences.
  • Continuity: the same contact knows your history and saves valuable time in an emergency.

Exactly this coordination — appointments, accompaniment, translation — is part of my private health companionship.

3. Organising medication and documents

Anyone with several doctors often has several medication lists — and no one looking at the whole picture. Abroad this becomes sharper: different brand names, different pack sizes, language barriers at the pharmacy. Interactions are easily overlooked this way.

A structured, up-to-date overview — what, why, how much, since when — is the simplest safety measure of all. In addition, keep a small folder: diagnoses, allergies, prior findings, contacts. Digital and on paper.

4. Preparing for an emergency — before it happens

Important: In an acute medical emergency on Mallorca, always dial 112 first — Europe-wide, free of charge, multilingual. No service and no app replaces the emergency response service.

Preparation does not mean fear, but calm. When documents, medication and contacts are in order, no one has to search in an emergency. This also includes the question: who is informed, who helps decide, when you cannot?

Modern early detection starts even earlier: Sensora Care combines vital-sign signals with interpretation, so that relevant changes become visible before they escalate — accompanied by an experienced person, not by an algorithm alone.

5. When the family is not on the island

Many clients live on Mallorca while their children are in Munich, Zurich or London. This distance is the real source of stress — for both sides. What helps: a shared point of contact on the ground, regular, honest updates and, where wished, a structured insight for relatives from afar. That way the family stays involved without living in constant worry.

6. Your checklist

  • Insurance cover checked (EHIC + private international/supplementary insurance if needed)?
  • A fixed point of contact defined for doctors, appointments and translation?
  • An up-to-date medication overview (what, why, how much) in place?
  • Emergency folder: diagnoses, allergies, prior findings, contacts — digital and on paper?
  • Is 112 known to everyone in the household as the first emergency number?
  • Decided who is informed and helps decide in an emergency?
  • For relatives far away: a route for updates / insight clarified?

Once you put these points in order, you win back the most valuable thing of all: calm. And the certainty that, in an emergency, everything is ready.
