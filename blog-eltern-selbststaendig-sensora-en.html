Guide · Care & Sensora

Let your parents live their lives. And you sleep soundly.

Angel GarciaICU nurse · 20+ years9 min read

Most people want two things for their parents that seem to contradict each other: that they live freely and independently — and that nothing happens to them. On Mallorca, with the family often far away, this balancing act becomes a daily worry. There is another way: care that works quietly in the background, and monitoring that does not patronise but protects.

1. The real wish: freedom without fear

Your parents have earned their life on the island — the calm, the light, their own rhythm. What no one wants: for that to turn into control, constant calls, the feeling of being watched. Good care begins exactly here — with respect for independence. It should not intrude on life, but safeguard it.

For you as a relative, that means: no longer worrying every day, no longer guessing from afar whether everything is fine. Instead, knowing that someone on the ground is paying attention — and who gets in touch before a small thing becomes an emergency.

2. What high-quality care truly takes off your shoulders

At the highest level, care does not mean "someone drops by". It means that an experienced person organises and leads the entire course of health matters — personally, discreetly, reliably:

  • Doctor and clinic coordination: the right specialists and private clinics, appointments, accompaniment, professional translation — instead of waiting lists and language barriers.
  • Medication overview: what, why, how much — and whether it all fits together. Questions are clarified directly with doctors and pharmacies.
  • Personal accompaniment: at examinations and hospital stays, at your parents' side — before, during and after.
  • One point of contact for the family: regular, honest updates. You have one number for everything.

All of this is the core of my private health companionship. Now a new layer is added that closes exactly the gap care alone cannot cover: the time between visits.

3. Sensora Care: seeing before it becomes urgent

With Sensora Care, your parent wears a discreet wearable — nothing more. In the background, relevant signals come together in a structured way: heart rate, oxygen saturation, activity and rest phases. Not as a flood of numbers, but as a basis for orientation — around the clock, at night too, even when no one is there.

The wearable measures. But readings alone are worthless if no one interprets them. That is exactly where the difference lies.

4. Why interpretation decides everything

A pulse that rises is, at first, just a number. If it rises because someone is climbing the stairs, that is completely normal — and it settles again shortly afterwards. That is healthy. A pure monitoring device might raise an alarm; we know there is no cause for concern here.

It becomes interesting with trends: when a pattern shows over hours that does not fit the situation — an oxygen saturation slowly drifting, a resting pulse staying unusually high or low across the day, markedly less activity than usual. Such courses can only be recognised with experience — and with an eye for the whole, not the single reading.

That experience is what I bring: more than twenty years of intensive-care and anaesthesia nursing, in which exactly this was daily work — recognising developments early, before they become serious. Sensora gives that experience a head start by making the signals visible. Patterns are also valuable for fall prevention: changed activity, more restlessness or suddenly more rest phases can be signs worth taking seriously and observing.

Important and honest: We do not make diagnoses and do not replace a doctor. Sensora is an orientation and early-detection system — not an emergency call system. In an acute medical emergency, always dial 112 first. What we do: recognise patterns, interpret them with nursing experience and initiate the right steps in good time.

5. The call that brings peace of mind

When something stands out, we do not wait — we get in touch. A calm call to check that everything is fine. Often it is harmless and quickly clarified. Sometimes it is the moment when acting early makes the difference: an appointment brought forward, an observation passed to the right place, a query that creates clarity.

For your parents, this means no restriction — they carry on living as before. For you, it means what is missing most when you are far away: calm. And, where you wish, a structured insight via the app, so you are involved without having to keep asking.

6. What a beginning looks like

It starts with a confidential conversation — about your situation, not about technology. Who is to be accompanied, what matters to you, how is the family involved? After that, I set everything up in person on site: wearable, app, access for the people you choose. Explained clearly, without hurdles. From then on, I keep an eye on the signals — and you can breathe easy.
